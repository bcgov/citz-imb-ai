Section 6 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

Procedure at meeting

6 The owners present at a general meeting shall choose a chairman for the meeting, who shall decide all questions of order and shall call for nominations of candidates for membership on the committee.

Section 7 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 112/2014, effective June 18, 2014.

Nominations for members

7 No person shall be nominated as a candidate where he refuses to accept the nomination.

Section 7 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

Nominations for members

7 No person shall be nominated as a candidate where he or she refuses to accept the nomination.

Section 9 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

Voting

9 Where more candidates are nominated than are required to fill the vacancies on the committee, the chairman shall, with the consent of the majority of owners present, declare when nominations are closed and shall then proceed to take the votes by ballot of the owners.

Section 10 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

Tied vote

10 In the event of a tied vote the chairman shall call for another vote on the candidates who are tied.

Section 14 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

Vote by agent

14 An owner may be represented by an agent who may vote for him.

Section 17 (1) and (2) BEFORE amended by BC Reg 76/2022, effective March 30, 2022.

(1) The committee shall appoint from among its members a chairman and may appoint a secretary treasurer who need not be a member.

(2) In case of an equality of votes, the chairman shall have a casting vote.

Section 20 BEFORE amended by BC Reg 112/2014, effective June 18, 2014.

Vacancy

20 (1) In the event of any casual vacancy in the membership of the committee the remaining members of the committee shall constitute the committee until the vacancy is filled.

(2) Where a member of the committee is absent from the Province for 6 months or fails to attend to his duties for a like period, the other members of the committee may declare his office vacant.

(3) A member of the committee ceases to hold office where

(a) he is convicted of an offence under the Act or regulations,

(b) by notice in writing to the committee he resigns his office, or

(c) his office is declared vacant by the other members of the committee.

(4) Where a vacancy occurs in the membership of the committee by reason of death or through a member ceasing to hold office, the remaining members of the committee, if constituted a quorum, may, subject to the approval of the minister, fill the vacancy, and the person appointed to fill the vacancy shall hold office for the unexpired term of the member whose office he fills.
