Land Act; Motor Vehicle (All Terrain) Act

Prohibition Regulation No. 4

[includes amendments up to B.C. Reg. 546/2004, December 31, 2004]

Whereas the use of all-terrain vehicles and snowmobiles has an adverse effect on wintering wildlife and their habitat within the Crown lands described in the schedule attached hereto:

Vehicle prohibition â€” December to March

1 Except for public officials in the discharge of their duties, the use of all-terrain vehicles and snowmobiles without the issuance of a permit is prohibited during the period December 1 to March 31 of each and every year on the Crown lands described on the schedule attached hereto.

Definitions

2 For the purpose of this regulation:

(a) "all-terrain vehicle" means any type of vehicle propelled by motorized power and capable of travel on or off a highway as defined in the Transportation Act ;

(b) "snowmobile" means a vehicle weighing not more than 1 000 pounds, designed primarily for travel on snow or ice, having one or more steering skis and self-propelled by means of an endless belt or belts driven in contact with the ground;

(c) "permit" is a permit issued by the Land Management Branch specifying terms and conditions for the use of Crown land.

[am. B.C. Reg. 546/2004, App. s. 17.]
