Forest Act

Annual Rent Regulation

[Last amended July 11, 2022 by B.C. Reg. 163/2022]

Definitions

1 In this regulation:

"Act" means the Forest Act ;

"reduced volume condition" means a condition on a licence that, under section 14 (1) (g.2) of the Act, reduces a portion of the allowable annual cut available to the holder of the licence;

"supplemental forest licence" has the same meaning as in section 14.2 (1) of the Act.

[en. B.C. Reg. 190/2014, Sch. 2, s. 1; am. B.C. Reg. 163/2022, s. (a).]

Annual rent

1.1 (1) For the purposes of sections 111 and 112 of the Act, the holder of a licence, agreement or permit described in Column 1 of the following table must pay to the government annual rent at the rate set out in Column 2 immediately opposite that licence, agreement or permit:

Item Column 1
Description of Licence,
Agreement or Permit 		Column 2
Rate of Annual Rent
1 Community forest agreement $0.37/m 3 of allowable annual cut determined for the community forest agreement area
2 First nations woodland licence $0.12/m 3 of allowable annual cut determined for the first nations woodland licence area
3 Forest licence, other than a supplemental forest licence $0.37/m 3 of allowable annual cut specified in the forest licence
4 Cutting permit issued under a supplemental forest licence $0.37/m 3 of volume of timber authorized to be harvested under the cutting permit
5 Timber licence $1.85/ha
6 Tree farm licence $0.57/m 3 of allowable annual cut determined for the tree farm licence area
7 Woodlot licence $0.60/m 3 of allowable annual cut determined for the woodlot licence area

(2) Despite subsection (1), the holder of a licence with a reduced volume condition must pay an amount that corresponds to the portion of the allowable annual cut that is not reduced by the reduced volume condition.

[en. B.C. Reg. 190/2014, Sch. 2, s. 1; am. B.C. Reg. 163/2022, s. (b).]

Woodlot Product Development Council payment

2 With respect to a woodlot licence, $0.25/m 3 of the rate set out in Column 2 of the table in section 1.1 is allocated under section 112.1 of the Act to represent money payable to the Woodlot Product Development Council.

[am. B.C. Reg. 190/2014, Sch. 2, s. 2.]
