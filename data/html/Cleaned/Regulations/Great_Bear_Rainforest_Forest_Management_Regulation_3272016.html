Great Bear Rainforest (Forest Management) Act

Great Bear Rainforest (Forest Management) Regulation

[includes amendments up to B.C. Reg. 124/2018, June 25, 2018]

Part 1 — Definitions and Interpretation

Definitions

1 In this regulation:

"AAC" means allowable annual cut;

"Act" means the Great Bear Rainforest (Forest Management) Act ;

"affected forest licence" has the same meaning as in section 26 (1) [definitions for Part 4] of the Act;

"GeoBC map" means a map deposited in the Media Vault, GeoBC, Victoria;

"TFL" means tree farm licence;

"TSA" means timber supply area.

[am. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 2.]

Interpretation for tables in sections 8, 12 and 16

1.1 (1) In an item of a table in section 8 (2) [AACs for forest licences in GBR North TSA] , 12 [AACs for forest licences in GBR South TSA] or 16 [AACs for forest licences in North Island TSA] :

"exempt forest licence" has the same meaning as in section 26 (1) of the Act;

"former TSA" , in relation to a forest licence, means the TSA that was specified in the licence, as that licence read on December 31, 2016;

"licence holder" , in relation to a forest licence, means the person that holds the licence as of January 1, 2017.

(2) In an item of a table referred to in subsection (1), an affected forest licence is identified by the following information:

(a) in column 1, the licence number of the licence;

(b) in column 2, the licence holder;

(c) in column 3, the licence's former TSA.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 3.]

Part 2 — Maximum AAC and Abolition of TSAs

Maximum AAC for GBR forest management area

2 The maximum AAC for the GBR forest management area is 2 500 000 m 3 .

Abolition of TSAs

3 The following TSAs are abolished:

(a) the North Coast TSA;

(b) the Mid Coast TSA;

(c) the Kingcome TSA;

(d) the Strathcona TSA.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 5.]

Part 3 — GBR North TSA

Designation of GBR North TSA

4 The land contained within the boundaries shown and described on the GeoBC map with the map name Great Bear Rainforest North Timber Supply Area and map reference number 577.3409711 G786 TSA1 2016 is designated as the GBR North TSA.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

AAC for GBR North TSA

5 The AAC for the Crown land in the GBR North TSA is 803 000 m 3 .

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

AAC partition for GBR North TSA

6 (1) The AAC of 803 000 m 3 specified for the GBR North TSA is partitioned as follows:

(a) 468 600 m 3 is attributable to those areas of Crown land that

(i) are located within the North Island Central Coast Forest District established under the Forest Act , and

(ii) are not located within the Owikeno Lake basin;

(b) 113 200 m 3 is attributable to western red cedar and yellow cedar located within the Coast Mountains Forest District established under the Forest Act .

(2) For the purposes of subsection (1) (a) (ii), the Owikeno Lake basin is the area located within the boundaries shown and labelled as the Owikeno Lake basin on the map referred to in section 4 [designation of GBR North TSA] .

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6; am. B.C. Reg. 124/2018, s. (c) (i).]

Part 8 — TFL 39

Division 1 — AAC for TFL Area for TFL 39

AAC for TFL area for TFL 39

23 The AAC for the TFL area for TFL 39 is

(a) 41 300 m 3 for the GBR part of the TFL area, and

(b) 1 375 000 m 3 for the non-GBR part of the TFL area.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Partition of AAC for TFL area for TFL 39

24 (1) In this section, "management plan" means a management plan within the meaning of section 35.2 [management plan for tree farm licence] of the Forest Act .

(2) The AAC of 1 375 000 m 3 specified for the non-GBR part of the TFL area for TFL 39 is partitioned in order to attribute 1 203 000 m 3 to those areas of Crown land and private land that are identified as conventional harvesting areas in the map attached to the management plan for the TFL area, as that management plan read on January 1, 2017.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Division 2 — Adaption of Cut Control for TFL 39

Definitions for Division 2

25 In this Division:

"last cut control period" , in relation to a notional licence, means the cut control period that ends when the AAC adjustment period ends;

"notional licence" means a notional licence established by section 27 [notional division of TFL 39] .

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Application of Division 2

26 This Division applies in respect of TFL 39 in order to take into account the following:

(a) the AAC specified by the Lieutenant Governor in Council for the GBR part of the TFL area;

(b) the AAC specified by the Lieutenant Governor in Council for the non-GBR part of the TFL area;

(c) if determined, the AAC determined by the chief forester for the non-GBR part of the TFL area.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6; am. B.C. Reg. 124/2018, s. (c) (ii).]

Notional division of TFL 39

27 (1) During the AAC adjustment period, TFL 39 is notionally divided into

(a) a notional licence for the GBR part of the TFL area, and

(b) a notional licence for the non-GBR part of the TFL area.

(2) A notional licence is deemed to be a licence as defined in section 75.4 (1) [cut control period for major licences] of the Forest Act .

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Cut control rules for TFL 39

28 (1) The first cut control period for a notional licence is deemed to have begun on the same day as the cut control period for TFL 39, as that cut control period existed immediately before January 1, 2017.

(2) Subject to subsections (1) and (3), the cut control periods for a notional licence are the cut control periods determined for the notional licence under section 75.4 [cut control period for major licences] of the Forest Act .

(3) The cut control period for a notional licence ends when the AAC adjustment period ends.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Assignment of timber harvested under TFL 39

29 During the AAC adjustment period, the minister may assign to a notional licence the volume of timber harvested under TFL 39 or portions of that volume that the minister considers appropriate.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]

Rules for excess volume harvested under TFL 39

30 If there is an excess volume of timber harvested during the last cut control period for a notional licence, the excess volume

(a) must be treated under section 75.7 [carry forward of excess harvest volume] of the Forest Act as being timber harvested during the next cut control period for TFL 39, and

(b) must not be offset by the unharvested volume, if any, relating to the last cut control period for the other notional licence.

[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
