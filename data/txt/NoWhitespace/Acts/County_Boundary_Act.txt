County boundary act [rsbc 1996] chapter 75 Division of british columbia into counties 1 For all purposes of the administration of justice, and if it is not Otherwise provided in an act relating to a special branch of it, and For all other purposes dealt with in any act if the provisions of this Act are incorporated or referred to, british columbia is divided into Divisions known and described as "counties".
Limits of counties bounded by water 2 (1) The limits of all counties bounded or partially bounded by the sea Extend to the boundary of the jurisdiction of the courts of british Columbia.
(2) The limits of all counties on any inlet, gulf or strait, lake, bay or River extend to the middle of the inlet, gulf or strait, lake or bay, And to the middle of the main channel of the river and, unless Otherwise provided, also include all the islands the whole or the Greater part of which are comprised within the outlines of the Counties prolonged.
Power of lieutenant governor to remedy and settle boundaries 3 (1) The lieutenant governor may by proclamation (a) Annex any small tract of land, not forming part of a county, to a County, or partly to one or more counties to which it may be adjacent, And
(b) Settle any doubt which may arise or exist in regard to the boundaries On the ground of a county.
(2) If the lieutenant governor exercises the power under subsection (1) (a), the tract then forms part of the county to which it is annexed.
(3) If the lieutenant governor exercises the power under subsection (1) (b), the boundaries are then the boundaries between the counties Affected for the distance specified.
Alteration: effect on process 4 (1) A sheriff, the boundaries of whose county are at any time altered, May, despite the alteration, proceed on and complete the enforcement Or service within the limits of the old county of any final process in The sheriff's hands at the time of the alteration, or of any renewal Of the process, or of any subsequent process in the same suit.
(2) The acts of the sheriff are valid to the same extent as if the Alteration had not been made.
