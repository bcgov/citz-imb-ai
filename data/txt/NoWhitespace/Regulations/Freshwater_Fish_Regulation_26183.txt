Wildlife Act
Freshwater Fish Regulation
[Last amended March 30, 2022 by B.C. Reg. 76/2022]
Interpretation
1
In this regulation,
"fish"
means a freshwater species of
(a)
Order
Petromyzoniformes
(lampreys),
(b)
Class
Osteichthyes
(bony fishes),
(c)
Class
Crustacea
(crustaceans), or
(d)
Class
Mollusca
and their eggs and juvenile stages but does not include
(e)
goldfish, or
(f)
species of ornamental tropical fishes.
[en. B.C. Reg. 145/2004, s. 1.]
Offences
2
A person commits an offence where the person
(a)
has in possession,
(b)
transports, or
(c)
traffics in
live fish unless authorized by a permit or a licence.
[am. B.C. Regs. 248/2001; 74/2017, App. 2.]
Authorization by permit
3
A regional manager or a person authorized by a regional manager may
issue a permit in accordance with this regulation on the terms and for
the period the regional manager or the person authorized by the
regional manager specifies authorizing a person to
(a)
possess,
(b)
transport, or
(c)
traffic in
live fish.
[en. B.C. Reg. 145/2004, s. 2; am. B.C. Reg. 76/2022, s. 3.]
Fee for authorization by permit
4
The fee for a permit issued under section 3 is $25 and the habitat
conservation trust fund surcharge to be paid isÂ $5.
[en. B.C. Reg. 145/2004, s. 2.]
