Part 1 — Definitions and Interpretation
Definitions
1
In this regulation:
"AAC"
means allowable annual cut;
"Act"
means the
Great Bear Rainforest (Forest Management) Act
;
"affected forest licence"
has the same meaning as in section 26 (1)
[definitions for Part 4]
of the Act;
"GeoBC map"
means a map deposited in the Media Vault, GeoBC, Victoria;
"TFL"
means tree farm licence;
"TSA"
means timber supply area.
[am. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 2.]
Interpretation for tables in sections 8, 12 and 16
1.1
(1)
In an item of a table in section 8 (2)
[AACs for forest licences in GBR North TSA]
, 12
[AACs for forest licences in GBR South TSA]
or 16
[AACs for forest licences in North Island TSA]
:
"exempt forest licence"
has the same meaning as in section 26 (1) of the Act;
"former TSA"
, in relation to a forest licence, means the TSA that was specified in
the licence, as that licence read on December 31, 2016;
"licence holder"
, in relation to a forest licence, means the person that holds the
licence as of January 1, 2017.
(2)
In an item of a table referred to in subsection (1), an affected
forest licence is identified by the following information:
(a)
in column 1, the licence number of the licence;
(b)
in column 2, the licence holder;
(c)
in column 3, the licence's former TSA.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 3.]

Division 2 — Adaption of Cut Control for TFL 39
Definitions for Division 2
25
In this Division:
"last cut control period"
, in relation to a notional licence, means the cut control period that
ends when the AAC adjustment period ends;
"notional licence"
means a notional licence established by section 27
[notional division of TFL 39]
.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
Application of Division 2
26
This Division applies in respect of TFL 39 in order to take into
account the following:
(a)
the AAC specified by the Lieutenant Governor in Council for the GBR
part of the TFL area;
(b)
the AAC specified by the Lieutenant Governor in Council for the
non-GBR part of the TFL area;
(c)
if determined, the AAC determined by the chief forester for the
non-GBR part of the TFL area.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6; am. B.C. Reg. 124/2018, s. (c)
(ii).]
Notional division of TFL 39
27
(1)
During the AAC adjustment period, TFL 39 is notionally divided into
(a)
a notional licence for the GBR part of the TFL area, and
(b)
a notional licence for the non-GBR part of the TFL area.
(2)
A notional licence is deemed to be a licence as defined in section
75.4 (1)
[cut control period for major licences]
of the
Forest Act
.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
Cut control rules for TFL 39
28
(1)
The first cut control period for a notional licence is deemed to have
begun on the same day as the cut control period for TFL 39, as that
cut control period existed immediately before January 1, 2017.
(2)
Subject to subsections (1) and (3), the cut control periods for a
notional licence are the cut control periods determined for the
notional licence under section 75.4
[cut control period for major licences]
of the
Forest Act
.
(3)
The cut control period for a notional licence ends when the AAC
adjustment period ends.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
Assignment of timber harvested under TFL 39
29
During the AAC adjustment period, the minister may assign to a
notional licence the volume of timber harvested under TFL 39 or
portions of that volume that the minister considers appropriate.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
Rules for excess volume harvested under TFL 39
30
If there is an excess volume of timber harvested during the last cut
control period for a notional licence, the excess volume
(a)
must be treated under section 75.7
[carry forward of excess harvest volume]
of the
Forest Act
as being timber harvested during the next cut control period for TFL
39, and
(b)
must not be offset by the unharvested volume, if any, relating to the
last cut control period for the other notional licence.
[en. B.C. Reg. 328/2016, Sch. s. 6.]
