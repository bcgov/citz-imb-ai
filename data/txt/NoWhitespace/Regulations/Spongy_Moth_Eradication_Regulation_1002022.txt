Link to consolidated regulation (pdf) Link to point in time Plant protection act Spongy moth eradication regulation [last amended april 24, 2023 by b.c. reg. 109/2023] Definitions
1 In this regulation:
"btk" Means an insecticide containing Bacillus thuringiensis Var.
Kurstaki
As its active ingredient; "buffer zone" Means the area shown in grey surrounding a treatment zone on the map In the schedule that relates to that treatment zone; "pesticide droplet monitoring card" Means a card which is used to determine the number, density and size Of droplets of pesticide spray deposited at a particular location; "spongy moth" Means the following species of moth:
(a) Lymantria albescens ; (b) Lymantria dispar ; (c) Lymantria dispar dispar ; (d) Lymantria umbrosa ; "treatment period" Means the period that begins april 1 and ends october 31; "treatment zone" Means a specified area outlined in heavy black on a map in the Schedule.
[am. b.c. reg. 109/2023, s. 2.] Powers of inspectors 2 (1) Subject to the limits set out in section 3, an inspector has the Following powers:
(a) To undertake treatment within a treatment zone by applying btk, using An aerial or ground spray; (b) To enter on land in a treatment zone to do any of the following:
(i) Search for and remove spongy moth eggs; (ii) Search for spongy moths; (iii) Set or retrieve traps for spongy moths; (c) To enter on land in a treatment zone or buffer zone to set or retrieve Pesticide droplet monitoring cards.
(2) For the purposes of subsection (1) (a), a treatment may consist of the Application of btk over multiple days if, due to mechanical, Meteorological or any other reason, btk cannot be applied throughout The treatment zone in a single day.
(3) Nothing in subsection (1) (a) limits an authorization granted under The
Integrated pest management act For overspray or drift from treatment to pass into the buffer zone.
[am. b.c. reg. 109/2023, s. 3.] Limits on powers 3 An inspector may exercise powers under section 2 only as follows:
(a) Btk may be applied in a treatment zone only during the treatment Period;
(b) No more than 4 treatments may be undertaken during the treatment Period;
(c) Land in a treatment zone may be entered for a purpose set out in Section 2 (1) (b) only during the treatment period; (d) Land in a treatment zone or buffer zone may be entered for a purpose Set out in section 2 (1) (c) only during the treatment period.
