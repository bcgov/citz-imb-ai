Balanced Budget and Ministerial Accountability Act
Minister of State for Child Care
Expected Results for the 2022/2023
Fiscal Year Regulation
Application of this regulation
1
This regulation applies to the Minister of State for Child Care for
the 2022/2023 fiscal year.
Definition
2
In this regulation,
"Act"
means the
Balanced Budget and Ministerial Accountability Act
.
Minister of State for Child Care
3
The expected results that the Minister of State for Child Care is to
achieve for the purposes of section 5 (3) of the Act are as follows:
(a)
continue to implement initiatives that support government's universal
child care plan that provides affordable, accessible, inclusive and
high-quality care and early learning for every child whose family
wants or needs it;
(b)
engage with the following to further inform implementation of
universal and inclusive child care:
(i)
all levels of government;
(ii)
Indigenous governing bodies and other entities representing Indigenous
peoples;
(iii)
school boards, child care providers and other sector stakeholders;
(c)
continue to support the transfer of responsibility for child care
delivery to the Ministry of Education with the goal of completing the
transfer by 2023;
(d)
continue to monitor the implementation of the Multilateral Early
Learning and Child Care Framework agreement with the federal
government;
(e)
submit to Cabinet a report on the results referred to in paragraphs
(a) through (d) on or before March 31, 2023.
[Provisions relevant to the enactment of this regulation:
Balanced Budget and Ministerial Accountability Act
, S.B.C. 2001, c. 28, s. 9.]
