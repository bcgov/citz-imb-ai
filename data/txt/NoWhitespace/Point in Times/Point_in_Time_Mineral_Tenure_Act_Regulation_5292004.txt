Section 20 before self-repeal, effective december 31, 2006.
Transitional provision: global positioning system survey 20 (1)  the cost of establishing the precision survey Coordinates of the legal posts of legacy mineral claims or legacy Placer claims using the global positioning system may be Registered as exploration and development if the following Conditions are met:
(a) the coordinates, corrected data and resulting map of the Surveyed claims comply with the standards and guidelines for Undertaking global positioning system surveys established by the Chief gold commissioner; (b) the data and map required in paragraph (a) are submitted To the chief gold commissioner as a report under section 33 Of the act within 30 days of registering the statement of Exploration and development.
(2)  a global positioning system survey of the position of a Legacy mineral claim or legacy placer claim may be recorded as Exploration and development only once during the life of the Claim.
(3)  this section is repealed on december 31, 2006.
