Greenhouse Gas Reduction Targets Act
Section 1, definition of "carbon neutral" BEFORE amended by
2014-29-58, effective January 1, 2016 (BC Reg 247/2015 as amended by
BC Reg 260/2015).
"carbon neutral"
, in relation to a public sector organization for a particular
period, means that the public sector organization has complied with
the obligations under section 6
[requirements for achieving carbon neutral status]
to
(a)
pursue actions to minimize the relevant greenhouse gas emissions for
that period, and
(b)
net those greenhouse gas emissions to zero in accordance with that
section;
Section 1, definition of "emission offset" BEFORE repealed by
2014-29-58, effective January 1, 2016 (BC Reg 247/2015 as amended by
BC Reg 260/2015).
"emission offset"
means an emission offset, as established, approved or recognized
under the regulations for the purpose of
(a)
reducing greenhouse gas emissions, or
(b)
reducing atmospheric greenhouse gas concentrations through storage,
sequestration or other means;
Section 1, definitions of "offset unit", "registry" and "retire"
were added by 2014-29-58, effective January 1, 2016 (BC Reg 247/2015
as amended by BC Reg 260/2015).
Section 1 definitions of "budget", "climate change risks", "fiscal
year", "motor vehicle" and "zero-emission vehicle" were added by
2019-43-1, effective November 28, 2019 (Royal Assent).

Progress reports on reducing BC greenhouse gas emissions
4
Beginning with a report on 2008 BC greenhouse gas emissions, and
continuing with a report for every subsequent even-numbered
calendar year, the minister must, as soon as reasonably
practicable for each year, make public a report respecting
(a)
a determination of the BC greenhouse gas emissions level for the
relevant calendar year,
(b)
the progress that has been made toward achieving the targets under
section 2,
(c)
the actions that have been taken to achieve that progress, and
(d)
the plans to continue that progress.
Part 1.1, section 4.1, was enacted by 2018-32-3, effective November
9, 2018 (BC Reg 235/2018).
Part 1.1, section 4.1, BEFORE repealed by 2019-43-4, effective
November 28, 2019 (Royal Assent).
Part 1.1 — Preparing for Climate Change
Reports on climate change risks and progress
4.1
Beginning with a report for the 2020 calendar year, and continuing
with a report for every subsequent even-numbered calendar year,
the minister must prepare and make public, as soon as reasonably
practicable for each year, a report respecting
(a)
a determination of the risks to BC that could reasonably be
expected to result from a changing climate,
(b)
the progress that has been made toward reducing those risks,
(c)
the actions that have been taken to achieve that progress, and
(d)
the plans to continue that progress.
Part 1.2, sections 4.2 and 4.3, was enacted by 2019-43-5, effective
November 28, 2019 (Royal Assent).
