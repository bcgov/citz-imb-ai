Plant Protection Act
North American Gypsy Moth Eradication Regulation, 2009
Definitions
1
In this regulation:
"Btk"
means the bacterial insecticide Bacillus thuringiensis var.
kurstaki;
"Harrison Spray Eradication Zone"
means the area outlined in black on the map referenced as the
Harrison Spray Eradication Zone in the Appendix to this
regulation;
"Harrison Trap Eradication Zone"
means the area outlined in black on the map referenced as the
Harrison Trap Eradication Zone in the Appendix to this regulation;
"gypsy moth"
means the North American gypsy moth.
Eradication of the gypsy moth
2
(1)  Subject to subsection (2), an inspector may do the
following in the Harrison Spray Eradication Zone during the period
beginning April 15, 2009, and ending June 20, 2009:
(a) enter on land in order to carry out an activity referred
to in paragraph (b) or (c);
(b) search for and remove gypsy moth eggs;
(c) apply Btk.
(2)  An inspector may apply Btk in a spray eradication zone
only if
(a) the application is done in accordance with the
Integrated Pest Management Act
and its regulations, and
(b) the inspector is licensed under that Act.
(3)  An inspector may do the following in the Harrison Trap
Eradication Zone during the period beginning June 15, 2009, and
ending October 30, 2009:
(a) enter on land in order to carry out an activity referred
to in paragraph (b) or (c);
(b) search for gypsy moths;
(c) set or retrieve traps for gypsy moths.
