Regulations in relation to claims of solicitor-client privilege 244 (1)  this section applies in relation to records (a) that are in the possession of a lawyer and that are about To be inspected, audited or examined under this act or about to be Seized under a warrant in relation to an offence under this act, And
(b) for which the lawyer at that time makes a claim of Solicitor-client privilege for a client of the lawyer in relation To the record.
(2)  the lieutenant governor in council may make regulations In relation to records referred to in subsection (1) as follows:
(a) establishing procedures for allowing a record to be Retained or seized and to be held in a secure manner until (i)  the claim of solicitor-client privilege is waived by the Client, or (ii)  the claim is determined, or the record is otherwise Dealt with, on application to the supreme court in accordance with The regulations; (b) establishing a right to apply to the supreme court to Resolve a claim of solicitor-client privilege regarding a record Retained or seized under paragraph (a) and establishing how the Court is to deal with such an application.
