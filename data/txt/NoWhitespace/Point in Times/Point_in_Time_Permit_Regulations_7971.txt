Section 3 before amended by bc reg 76/2022, effective march 30, 2022.
Application for grazing permit 3 Any person desiring to use the management area range for grazing In any year must make application to the creston valley wildlife Management authority for a grazing permit entitling him to Depasture cattle thereon, subject to the provisions of the Creston valley wildlife act And this regulation and the terms and conditions of the grazing Permit.
Section 5 (b) before amended by bc reg 76/2022, effective march 30, 2022.
(b) The permittee is forced to temporarily reduce his rate of stocking Due to some physical disaster completely beyond his control, and Directly affecting his livestock or the forage producing ability of His ranch; Section 7 before amended by bc reg 76/2022, effective march 30, 2022.
Cattle to be counted and identified 7 The applicant or holder of a grazing permit may be required to Make satisfactory provision for having all his cattle counted and Identification checked by the management authority before they are Turned out on the management area, and the issuance of the grazing Permit may be withheld until such provision for count and Identification is satisfactorily completed.
Section 20 before amended by bc reg 76/2022, effective march 30, 2022.
Reduction or rebate in fees 20 No reduction or rebate in fees is made unless, in the opinion of The management authority, the holder of the permit is prevented From depasturing animals or cutting hay pursuant to the permit by Circumstances beyond his control, nor unless application in Writing for the reduction or rebate is made within one week after Such circumstances have arisen.
Section 22 (a), (b), (c), (d) and (g) before amended by bc reg 76/2022, effective march 30, 2022.
