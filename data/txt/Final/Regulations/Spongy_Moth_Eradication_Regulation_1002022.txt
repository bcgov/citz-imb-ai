Plant Protection Act




Spongy Moth Eradication Regulation



[Last amended April 24, 2023 by B.C. Reg. 109/2023]









Definitions





1



In this regulation:



"Btk"

means an insecticide containing

Bacillus thuringiensis

var.

kurstaki

as its active ingredient;



"buffer zone"

means the area shown in grey surrounding a treatment zone on the map in the Schedule that relates to that treatment zone;



"pesticide droplet monitoring card"

means a card which is used to determine the number, density and size of droplets of pesticide spray deposited at a particular location;



"spongy moth"

means the following species of moth:






(a)



Lymantria albescens

;






(b)



Lymantria dispar

;






(c)



Lymantria dispar dispar

;






(d)



Lymantria umbrosa

;



"treatment period"

means the period that begins April 1 and ends October 31;



"treatment zone"

means a specified area outlined in heavy black on a map in the Schedule.


[am. B.C. Reg. 109/2023, s. 2.]








Powers of inspectors





2



(1)


Subject to the limits set out in section 3, an inspector has the following powers:






(a)


to undertake treatment within a treatment zone by applying Btk, using an aerial or ground spray;






(b)


to enter on land in a treatment zone to do any of the following:






(i)


search for and remove spongy moth eggs;






(ii)


search for spongy moths;






(iii)


set or retrieve traps for spongy moths;






(c)


to enter on land in a treatment zone or buffer zone to set or retrieve pesticide droplet monitoring cards.






(2)


For the purposes of subsection (1) (a), a treatment may consist of the application of Btk over multiple days if, due to mechanical, meteorological or any other reason, Btk cannot be applied throughout the treatment zone in a single day.






(3)


Nothing in subsection (1) (a) limits an authorization granted under the


Integrated Pest Management Act


for overspray or drift from treatment to pass into the buffer zone.


[am. B.C. Reg. 109/2023, s. 3.]








Limits on powers





3



An inspector may exercise powers under section 2 only as follows:






(a)


Btk may be applied in a treatment zone only during the treatment period;






(b)


no more than 4 treatments may be undertaken during the treatment period;






(c)


land in a treatment zone may be entered for a purpose set out in section 2 (1) (b) only during the treatment period;






(d)


land in a treatment zone or buffer zone may be entered for a purpose set out in section 2 (1) (c) only during the treatment period.
