Enrollment limited by district



4

(1)  The notarial districts listed in the first column of the
Schedule are established and the number of members who may be
enrolled to practice within a district is limited to the number
set out in the third column of the Schedule.


(2)  A notarial district includes the areas listed in the
second column of the Schedule as those areas are incorporated or,
in the case of unincorporated areas, as they were known on
August 21, 1981.

Application for enrollment




5

(1)


A Canadian citizen or permanent resident of Canada may, on payment
of the prescribed fee, apply to the court under the Supreme Court
Civil Rules for enrollment as a member.




(2)

An application for enrollment as a member must be filed in the
Vancouver registry and heard at Vancouver.




(3)

At least 30 days before the hearing of an application for
enrollment as a member a copy of the application must be served on
the secretary of the society and on the executive director of the
Law Society of British Columbia.




(4)

At least 30 days before the hearing of an application for
enrollment as a member, a notice of the application and the date
of the hearing must be published in a manner determined by the
society.




(5)

A person may oppose the application at the hearing, if the person
serves notice of the person's opposition on the applicant and
files a copy of the notice with the registrar.

Enrollment for area outside district



7

(1)  If the court is satisfied that there is need of a notary
public in an area of British Columbia not within a notarial
district and that an applicant is a fit person for enrollment as a
member for the area, it may order that the applicant be examined
in the duties of a notary public and, if found qualified on the
examination, be enrolled as a member.


(2)  An order under subsection (1) must define the area for
which the applicant may be enrolled and, on being enrolled, to
which the applicant's practice is limited.






Section 8 BEFORE repealed by 2008-39-59, effective January 1, 2009
(BC Reg 325/2008).




Transfer between notarial districts



8

(1)  On payment of the prescribed fee, a member may apply to
the court under the Rules of Court to change the notarial district
to which the member's practice is limited under
section 6 (2) to another notarial district in which a
vacancy exists.


(2)  An application under subsection (1) must be accompanied
by an affidavit of the secretary stating that


(a) a vacancy exists in the notarial district to which the
member wishes to transfer, and

(b) the directors consent to the transfer.
