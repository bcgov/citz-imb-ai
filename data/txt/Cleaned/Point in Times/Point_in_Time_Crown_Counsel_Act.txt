Section 7 (1), (2) and (5) BEFORE amended by 2023-10-160, effective March 30, 2023 (Royal Assent).






(1)


If the ADAG considers it is in the public interest, he or she may appoint a lawyer, who is not employed in the Ministry of Attorney General, as a special prosecutor.






(2)


A special prosecutor must carry out his or her mandate, as set out in writing by the ADAG, and in particular must






(a)


examine all relevant information and documents and report to the ADAG with respect to the approval and conduct of any specific prosecution, and






(b)


carry out any other responsibilities respecting the initiation and conduct of a specific prosecution.






(5)


Subject to the mandate given to the special prosecutor by the ADAG or to a directive referred to in subsection (4), the decision of a special prosecutor with respect to any matter within his or her mandate is final, but a decision not to approve a prosecution may be appealed by a law enforcement officer under the process established by section 4 (4).
