Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
Prescribed Health Care Profession Regulation
Midwifery
1
Midwifery is a prescribed health care profession for the purposes
of the definition of "practitioner" in section 1 of the
Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
.
Pharmacy
2
Pharmacy is a prescribed health care profession for the purposes
of the definition of "practitioner" in section 1 of the
Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
only for the purposes of prescribing the following drugs for use
for emergency contraception:
(a) Ethinyl estradiol;
(b) Repealed. [B.C. Reg. 384/2007.]
(c) Norgestrel;
(d) Progestin.
[en. B.C. Reg. 354/2000; am. B.C. Reg. 384/2007.]
Nursing as a nurse practitioner
3
Nursing as a nurse practitioner is a prescribed health care
profession for the purposes of the definition of "practitioner" in
section 1 of the
Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
.
[en. B.C. Reg. 232/2005, App. s. 12.]
Optometry
4
Optometry is a prescribed health care profession for the purposes
of the definition of "practitioner" in section 1 of the
Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
.
[en. B.C. Reg. 422/2008, App. s. 8.]
[Provisions of the
Pharmacists, Pharmacy Operations and Drug Scheduling Act
, R.S.B.C. 1996, c. 363, relevant to the enactment of this
regulation: section 1]
