Section 1 definition of "electronic logging device" or "ELD" BEFORE
amended by BC Reg 39/2023, effective August 1, 2023.
"electronic logging device"
or
"ELD"
means a device or technology that
(a)
automatically records a driver's driving time,
(b)
facilitates the recording of the driver's record of duty status, and

Regulations (Canada);
Section 1 definitions of "driving time" and "record of duty status"

"driving time"
has the same meaning as in section 37.01 of the Motor Vehicle
Act Regulations, B.C. Reg. 26/58;
"record of duty status"
means a record in which a driver records the information required
under section 77 or 82 of the Commercial Vehicle Drivers Hours
of Service Regulations (Canada).

Persons engaged in prescribed activity — commercial motor vehicle
with ELD
5.1
For the purposes of section 214.5 (b) of the Act, a
person who is driving or operating a commercial motor vehicle, if
the commercial motor vehicle is equipped with an electronic
logging device that is mounted in a fixed position during the
operation of the commercial motor vehicle and is visible to the
driver when the driver is in the normal driving position, is
engaged in a prescribed activity and the person is exempt from the
prohibition in section 214.2 of the Act in respect of the use
of the electronic logging device.
[en. B.C. Reg. 39/2023, Sch. 1, s. 3.]
