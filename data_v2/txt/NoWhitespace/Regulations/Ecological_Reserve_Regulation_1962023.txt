Ecological Reserve Act
Ecological Reserve Regulation
Definitions
1
In this regulation:
"Act"
means the
Ecological Reserve Act
;
"activated e-cigarette"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Tobacco and Vapour Products Control Act
;
"aircraft"
means a device in, on or by which a person is transported or drawn by
air, whether using a motorized or non-motorized means of propulsion,
and includes fixed or rotary wing airplanes and gliders;
"camp"
has the same meaning as in section 1 (1) of the Park,
Conservancy and Recreation Area Regulation;
"cannabis"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Cannabis Control and Licensing Act
;
"domesticated animal"
means an animal that is
(a)
tame,
(b)
under the care or control of a person, or
(c)
kept by a person for a commercial purpose;
"drone"
means an aerial device that
(a)
is not occupied or operated by a person within the device, and
(b)
is operated by one or more of the following:
(i)
remote control;
(ii)
pre-programmed software;
(iii)
onboard computer autonomy or artificial intelligence;
"e-cigarette"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Tobacco and Vapour Products Control Act
;
"e-substance"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Tobacco and Vapour Products Control Act
;
"fish"
includes the following:
(a)
a vertebrate of the order
Petromyzontiformes
(lampreys) or class
Osteichthyes
(bony fishes);
(b)
an invertebrate of the phylum
Echinodermata
(echinoderms), class
Crustacea
(crustaceans) or class
Mollusca
(mollusks);
(c)
any other aquatic animal, except a bird or mammal;
(d)
an egg or juvenile stage of an animal described in any of
paragraphs (a) to (c);
"hunt"
has the same meaning as in section 1 (1) of the Park,
Conservancy and Recreation Area Regulation;
"motorized boat"
means a device in, on or by which a person is transported or drawn on
water using a motorized means of propulsion;
"natural resources"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Park Act
;
"non-motorized boat"
means a device in, on or by which a person is transported or drawn on
water using a non-motorized means of propulsion;
"tobacco"
has the same meaning as in section 1 of the
Tobacco and Vapour Products Control Act
;
"trap"
has the same meaning as in section 1 (1) of the Park,
Conservancy and Recreation Area Regulation;
"vehicle"
means a device in, on or by which a person or thing is transported or
drawn on land, whether using a motorized or non-motorized means of
propulsion;
"waste"
has the same meaning as in section 1 (1) of the
Environmental Management Act
;
"wildlife"
has the same meaning as in section 1 (1) of the Park,
Conservancy and Recreation Area Regulation.
Ecological reserve entry, use and occupation restricted
2
(1)
Subject to this section, a person must not enter, use or occupy an
ecological reserve unless authorized by a permit issued under
section 5.1 (1) of the Act.
(2)
A person may, without a permit, enter and travel in or through, by
foot or by non-motorized boat, an ecological reserve other than an
ecological reserve set out in the Schedule.
(3)
Subsection (2) does not authorize entry to an ecological reserve
if a heat or rainfall warning issued by Environment and Climate Change
Canada is in effect in relation to an area that encompasses, overlaps
with or abuts the ecological reserve.
(4)
A permittee must comply with the terms and conditions included in the
permittee's permit under section 5.1 (5) of the Act.
Notice of conditions
3
The minister must, if satisfied that the circumstances set out in
section 2 (3) exist in relation to an ecological reserve,
provide public notice, in the manner the minister considers
appropriate, that entry to the ecological reserve is prohibited under
section 2 unless authorized by a permit.
Application for permit
4
(1)
For the purposes of section 5.1 (1) of the Act, a person may
apply to the minister for a permit authorizing the use and occupation
of an ecological reserve for ecological scientific research or
educational purposes.
(2)
An application for a permit must include the following:
(a)
a description of the proposed scientific research or educational uses
and activities;
(b)
a description and map of the areas of land that would be affected by
the uses and activities;
(c)
a description of the proposed means of entry to and access through the
ecological reserve;
(d)
the proposed duration of the uses and activities;
(e)
the expected ecological impact of the uses and activities;
(f)
the number of individuals proposed to enter the ecological reserve
and, if known, their names;
(g)
the name of the individual proposed to direct the uses and activities.
Hearing
5
(1)
The minister is not required to hold a hearing before suspending,
modifying or cancelling a permit.
(2)
If the minister holds a hearing, it may be held in writing,
electronically or in person, or by any combination of written,
electronic or in-person hearings.
Prohibitions
6
(1)
A person must not do or attempt to do any of the following in an
ecological reserve:
(a)
prospect or explore for, or extract, coal, petroleum or natural gas;
(b)
drill for or divert water or dam a waterway.
(2)
A person must not do or attempt to do any of the following unless
authorized to do so under a permit issued under
section 5.1 (1) of the Act:
(a)
introduce a plant or animal species in an ecological reserve;
(b)
operate a vehicle or a motorized boat, or conduct a take-off or
landing of an aircraft or a drone, in an ecological reserve;
(c)
deposit waste in an ecological reserve;
(d)
allow or cause waste to flow onto or seep into land in an ecological
reserve or to flow or seep into water in an ecological reserve;
(e)
allow or cause waste to be emitted into the air from within an
ecological reserve;
(f)
mine or quarry earth, soil, peat, marl, sand, gravel, rock or another
mineral resource in an ecological reserve;
(g)
make an alteration to land on or beneath its surface in an ecological
reserve;
(h)
damage, destroy or take a sample of a natural resource in, or remove a
natural resource from, an ecological reserve;
(i)
disturb an animal in an ecological reserve;
(j)
hunt or trap in, catch fish in, or take fish or wildlife from, an
ecological reserve;
(k)
touch or feed wildlife in an ecological reserve or entice wildlife in
an ecological reserve to approach by any means, including by holding
out or setting out a decoy, lure, food or bait or by using a wildlife
caller;
(l)
light, fuel or use a fire in an ecological reserve;
(m)
allow a domesticated animal to graze in an ecological reserve;
(n)
cut or fell timber in an ecological reserve;
(o)
construct or modify a road or trail in an ecological reserve;
(p)
camp in an ecological reserve;
(q)
establish, construct or modify a building or other structure in an
ecological reserve;
(r)
smoke cannabis, or hold lighted cannabis, in an ecological reserve;
(s)
use or hold, in an ecological reserve, an activated e-cigarette that
is vapourizing cannabis;
(t)
smoke tobacco, or hold lighted tobacco, in an ecological reserve;
(u)
use or hold, in an ecological reserve, an activated e-cigarette that
is vapourizing an e-substance other than cannabis.
(3)
This section does not apply in relation to a use or activity carried
out
(a)
under section 18 of the
Wildfire Act
, or
(b)
by or on behalf of the minister for the purposes of one or more of the
following:
(i)
restoring an ecosystem;
(ii)
protecting a rare or endangered native plant or animal;
(iii)
carrying out ecological scientific research or education.
Offences
7
A person who contravenes any of the following commits an offence:
(a)
section 2 (1) or (4);
(b)
section 6 (1) or (2).
