Financial Institutions Act
Shared Premises Regulation
[includes amendments up to B.C. Reg. 577/2004, December 31, 2004]
Interpretation
1
The definitions in section 168 of the
Financial Institutions Act
apply for the purposes of this regulation.
Conditions respecting business premises of trust companies and credit
unions
2
(1)
Subject to subsections (2) and (3), it is a condition of every
business authorization issued to a trust company, credit union or
extraprovincial trust corporation that its business office be located
in premises that are separate and distinct from the business office of
an insurance agent.
(2)
A business office of a credit union that, immediately before
September 15, 1990, was located in the same premises as a
business office of an insurance agent may continue to be located in
those premises if the business office of the credit union is in an
area of the premises that is separate and distinct from the business
office of the insurance agent.
(3)
Where a credit union relocates a business office described in
subsection (2), the business office of the insurance agent described
in subsection (2) may be relocated to the same premises if the
business office of the credit union is in an area of the premises that
is separate and distinct from the business office of the insurance
agent.
[am. B.C. Reg. 181/92.]
Conditions respecting business premises of insurance agents
3
(1)
It is a condition of every licence issued to an insurance agent, other
than an insurance agent described in subsection (2) or (3), that the
business office of the insurance agent be located in premises that are
separate and distinct from the business office of a savings
institution.
(2)
A business office of an insurance agent that, immediately before
September 15, 1990, was located in the same premises as a business
office of a credit union, may continue in those premises if the
business office of the insurance agent is in an area of the premises
that is separate and distinct from the area occupied by the business
office of the credit union.
(3)
If a credit union relocates a business office described in subsection
(2), the insurance agent described in subsection (2) may relocate its
business office to the same premises, if the business office of the
insurance agent is in an area of the new premises that is separate and
distinct from the area occupied by the business office of the credit
union.
[en. B.C. Reg. 577/2004.]
