Health Professions Act


Physical Therapists Regulation





Definitions



1

In this regulation:


"physical therapy"
means the treatment of the human body by physical or mechanical
means, by manipulation, massage, exercise, the application of
bandages, hydrotherapy, and medical electricity, for the
therapeutic purpose of maintaining or restoring function that has
been impaired by injury or disease.





Designation



2

(1)  Physical therapy is designated as a health profession.


(2)  The "College of Physical Therapists of British Columbia"
is the name of the college established under section 15 (1) of the
Health Professions Act
for physical therapy.





Reserved titles



3

No person other than a registrant may use the title "physical
therapist", "registered physical therapist", "physiotherapist",
"registered physiotherapist", "remedial gymnast" or "registered
remedial gymnast".





Scope of practice



4

A registrant may practise physical therapy.





Reserved acts



5

(1)  Subject to section 14 of the
Health Professions Act
, no person other than a registrant may practise physical therapy.


(2)  Subsection (1) does not apply to a person employed by
and on the premises of a hospital which has been designated by the
minister and who is acting under the direction of a medical
practitioner.





Limitation on practice



6

No registrant may prescribe or administer drugs or anaesthetics,
or treat a recent fracture of a bone, except under the direction
of a medical practitioner.





Patient relations program



7

The college is designated for the purposes of section 16 (2) (f)
of the
Health Professions Act
.





Health profession corporations



8

Part 4 of the
Health Professions Act
applies to physical therapy.
