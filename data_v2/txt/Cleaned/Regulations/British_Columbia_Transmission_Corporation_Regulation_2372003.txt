Financial Administration Act



British Columbia Transmission

Corporation Regulation






Definition




1


In this regulation,
"transmission corporation"
means the British Columbia Transmission Corporation, a company
incorporated under the

Business Corporations Act

.






Fiscal agent




2


The Minister of Finance is appointed as fiscal agent for the
transmission corporation.






Powers of fiscal agent




3


As fiscal agent for the transmission corporation, the Minister of
Finance may, at the request of the transmission corporation, arrange
all details and do, transact and execute all deeds, matters and things
necessary or advisable for the transmission corporation to engage in
the activities described in section 4.






Prohibited activities




4


Despite any Act, the transmission corporation may not, without the
approval of the Minister of Finance, do any of the following:




(a)

borrow money;




(b)

issue, purchase, exchange or dispose of securities in relation to its
borrowings;




(c)

enter into interest rate or currency exchange agreements or other
derivative transactions in relation to its borrowings, lendings or
investments.



[Provisions of the

Financial Administration Act

, R.S.B.C. 1996, c. 138, relevant to the enactment of this
regulation: section 75]
