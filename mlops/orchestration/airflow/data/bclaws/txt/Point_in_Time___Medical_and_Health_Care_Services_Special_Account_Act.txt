Medical and Health Care Services Special Account Act
[RSBC 1996] CHAPTER
284
Definitions
1
(1) 							In this Act:
"agreement"
means an agreement made under section 5 of the
Medicare Protection Act
with a corporation representing members of a profession;
"appropriation"
means appropriation as defined in the
Medicare Protection Act
;
"minister"
means the minister charged with the administration of the
Medicare Protection Act
;
"prescribed group"
means the group of practitioners prescribed under section 2 (2);
"sub-account"
means an account established under section 2 (2) within the Medical and Health Care Services Special Account.
(2)
Subject to subsection (1), the definitions in section 1 of the
Financial Administration Act
, and in sections 1 and 23 of the
Medicare Protection Act
, apply to this Act.
Special account and sub-accounts
2
(1)
The Medical and Health Care Services Special Account is established as a special account.
(2)
The Minister of Finance, on the recommendation of the minister, may establish a sub-account to reduce fluctuations over 2 or more fiscal years in demand for payment from appropriations for a prescribed group of practitioners.
(3)
The minister may specify the name of a sub-account.
Composition of a sub-account
3
A sub-account consists of
(a) the portion, specified by Treasury Board on the recommendation of the minister for the purposes of the sub-account, of an appropriation,
(b) the unexpended portion, or a prescribed lesser portion, of the available amount at the end of the fiscal year for the prescribed group to which the sub-account applies, and
(c) amounts approved for the purposes of the sub-account by the Lieutenant Governor in Council and recovered under section 37 (1) (d) of the
Medicare Protection Act
for the prescribed group to which the sub-account applies.
Expenditures out of a sub-account
4
On the written authorization of the minister, amounts may be paid out of a sub-account
(a) as provided for under the applicable agreement, or
(b) if paragraph (a) does not apply, as prescribed by the regulations for the reduction of fluctuations over 2 or more fiscal years in demand for payment from appropriations.
Power to make regulations
5
The Lieutenant Governor in Council may make regulations referred to in section 41 of the
Interpretation Act
.
