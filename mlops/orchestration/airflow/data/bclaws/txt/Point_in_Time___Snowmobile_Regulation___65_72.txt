Division 1 — Interpretation
Definitions
1
In this regulation, unless the context otherwise requires,
"Act"
means the
Motor Vehicle (All Terrain) Act
;
"competition snowmobile"
means a snowmobile designed for use, and used exclusively, on competition circuits;
"owner's certificate"
means a certificate of registration issued to an owner of a snowmobile under section 2 of the Act before its repeal;
"owner's decal"
means the method of identification of a snowmobile under section 2 of the Act before its repeal;
"snowmobile"
means a vehicle weighing not more than 1 000 pounds, designed primarily for travel on snow or ice, having one or more steering skis, and self-propelled by means of an endless belt or belts driven in contact with the ground; and includes a snowmobile conversion vehicle, but does not include a competition snowmobile;
"snowmobile conversion vehicle"
means a vehicle designed to be capable of conversion to a snowmobile by the repositioning, removal or addition of parts.
[am. B.C. Reg. 200/2014, App. 2, s. 1.]

Division 4 — Rental Dealer Registration
Registration
14
(1)
Except as otherwise provided in the Act or this regulation, every dealer shall, before any rental snowmobile owned by him or in his charge is used or operated, obtain a rental dealer's certificate.
(2)
A dealer must
(a)
make application for registration and a rental dealer's certificate in a form satisfactory to the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia,
(b)
state the number of rental snowmobiles owned by the dealer or in the dealer's charge, or anticipated to be owned by the dealer or in the dealer's charge, and
(c)
deliver the application to the corporation or a government agent or any person authorized under section 3 (5) or (6) of the
Motor Vehicle Act
for that purpose, accompanied by a fee prescribed in Schedule 1 for the registration.
(3)
Upon the receipt of the application and upon being satisfied of the truth of the facts stated in the application and that the prescribed fees have been paid, the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia shall register the name and address of the dealer in a file or index to be kept for that purpose, and shall issue to the dealer a rental dealer's certificate authorizing the use and operation by the dealer of 5 rental snowmobiles shown on the certificate, together with sufficient duplicate rental dealer's certificates and rental dealer's plates for each rental snowmobile of that number.
(4)
Where a rental dealer applies for registration of more than 5 rental snowmobiles, the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia may, in accordance with this section and upon receipt of a fee prescribed in Schedule 1 for each additional rental snowmobile, issue additional rental dealer's certificates, duplicate rental dealer's certificates and rental dealer's plates.
(5)
Every rental dealer's certificate, duplicate rental dealer's certificate and rental dealer's plate shall expire on September 30, and shall be of such material and design as the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia may determine, and a rental dealer's plate shall remain the property of the Crown.
[am. B.C. Regs. 403/95, ss. 4, 5; 415/97, ss. 1, 4.]
Rental dealer's plate
15
Every rental snowmobile owned by a dealer or in his charge, while it is being used or operated, shall have affixed to some conspicuous place on it a rental dealer's plate.
Additional certificates and plates
16
Upon receipt of an application in writing accompanied by such evidence as the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia may require, and upon payment of a fee prescribed in Schedule 1 for each additional rental dealer's plate, the corporation may issue an additional rental dealer's certificate and additional duplicate rental dealer's certificates and rental dealer's plates.
[am. B.C. Regs. 403/95, s. 6; 415/97, s. 1.]
Inspection and seizure of plate
17
(1)
Every dealer and every user or operator of a rental snowmobile shall, upon demand, produce a duplicate rental dealer's certificate showing the rental snowmobile that he is using or operating, for inspection by any enforcement officer.
(2)
An enforcement officer may remove and seize a rental dealer's plate that he finds detached from a rental snowmobile, or displayed on a snowmobile other than a rental snowmobile owned by the dealer or in his charge, and in respect of which the rental dealer's certificate was issued, and may hold the same pending the receipt of instructions from the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia as to its disposal.
[am. B.C. Reg. 415/97, s. 1.]
Liability insurance
18
Before registering a dealer and issuing a rental dealer's certificate, the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia may make such inquiries and require such information as the corporation deems desirable, and shall require proof of liability insurance of at least $50 000 for the purpose of satisfying liabilities the dealer may incur resulting from bodily injury to or the death of any person, or damage to property occasioned by or arising out of the ownership, operation or use of a rental snowmobile owned by the dealer or in his charge.
[am. B.C. Reg. 415/97, s. 5.]
